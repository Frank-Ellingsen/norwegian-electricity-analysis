# 🚀 Project Launch: Norwegian Electricity Spot Price Analysis (2021-2026)

Why does electricity in Southern Norway cost up to 3x more than in the North? 🇳🇴⚡

A dive into the Norwegian electricity market, and the data tells a fascinating story of regional divergence, grid constraints, and seasonal volatility.

### 🔍 The Challenge
The Norwegian market is split into 5 bidding zones (NO1-NO5). While the North (NO4) enjoys stable, low prices driven by surplus hydropower, the South (NO1, NO2, NO5) faces extreme volatility and higher averages due to European market exposure and grid bottlenecks.

### 💡 Key Findings
*   **The Divide:** Average South prices reached **90.09 EUR/MWh** vs. **31.34 EUR/MWh** in the North.
*   **Volatility:** Winter spikes in the South frequently exceeded **800 EUR/MWh**.
*   **Convergence:** Grid upgrades and high renewable output in Europe are slowly closing the gap.

### 🛠️ The Tech Stack
*   **Data Engineering:** Python (Pandas) + SQLite for automated ETL from ENTSO-E and Nord Pool APIs.
*   **Analytics:** Time-series decomposition (Trend, Seasonal, Residual) using Statsmodels.
*   **Visualization:** Power BI for interactive dashboarding and regional comparison.
